Break-Fix vs. Managed IT: Why "Calling the Computer Guy" is Costing You Money

If you run a business in Bergen County, you likely handle IT in one of two ways.

1. The Break-Fix Model: You wait until a server smokes or email stops working, then you frantically call a repair shop. You pay a high hourly rate, your employees sit idle for hours, and you lose revenue.

2. The Managed Services Model (MSP): You pay a flat monthly fee (often less than a car lease) to have a professional team monitor your systems 24/7/365.

The Hidden Cost of Downtime

Most business owners look at the monthly invoice of Managed IT and hesitate. But they forget to calculate the cost of downtime. The Incentive Problem

In a Break-Fix relationship, your IT guy makes money when you are in pain. Their incentive is to apply a "band-aid" fix and hope it breaks again in six months. In a Managed Services relationship, we are incentivized to keep you running perfectly. If you have an issue, it costs us money to fix it.

What Does Managed IT Include?

  • Vendor Management: Internet down? We sit on hold with Verizon so you don't have to.
  • Patch Management: We update your software automatically at night so it doesn't disrupt your day.
