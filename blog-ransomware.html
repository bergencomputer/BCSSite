Why Small Businesses in NJ are the #1 Target for Ransomware

Many business owners in Bergen County operate under a dangerous myth: "I'm too small to be hacked. Why would they target my flower shop or accounting firm?"

The reality is terrifying. 43% of all cyber attacks target small businesses. Why? Because major corporations like Chase Bank or Target have spent millions on fortresses of security. Small businesses, on the other hand, often leave the digital front door unlocked.

The "Low Hanging Fruit" Strategy

Hackers use automated bots to scan the internet for vulnerabilities. They aren't specifically looking for you; they are looking for anyone with an unpatched Windows server or a weak firewall. Once they get in, they deploy ransomware that encrypts your client data and demands payment (usually $10,000 to $50,000) to unlock it.

How to Protect Your NJ Business

Standard antivirus is no longer enough. Modern threats are "file-less" and can slip past Norton or McAfee. Here is the security stack we recommend for all our clients in Ramsey and Paramus:

  • Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR): Unlike antivirus, EDR looks for suspicious behavior. If a program suddenly tries to encrypt 1,000 files in 5 seconds, EDR kills it instantly.
  • Immutable Backups: Hackers try to delete your backups first. We use "immutable" cloud storage that literally cannot be deleted or altered by anyone—even us—for a set period.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): 99% of password hacks are stopped simply by requiring a code from your phone.
