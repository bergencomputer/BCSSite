Why Small Businesses in NJ are the #1 Target for Ransomware

Many business owners in Bergen County operate under a dangerous myth: "I'm too small to be hacked. Why would they target my flower shop, law firm, or accounting office?"

The reality is terrifying. 43% of all cyber attacks target small businesses. Why? Because major corporations like Chase Bank or Target have spent millions on fortresses of security. Small businesses, on the other hand, often leave the digital front door unlocked.

The "Low Hanging Fruit" Strategy

Hackers use automated bots to scan the internet for vulnerabilities. They aren't specifically looking for you; they are looking for anyone with an unpatched Windows server, a weak RDP password, or an outdated firewall. Once they get in, they deploy ransomware that encrypts your client data and demands payment (usually $10,000 to $50,000 in Bitcoin) to unlock it.

Why Antivirus is Not Enough

Ten years ago, installing Norton or McAfee was enough. Today, it is useless against modern threats. Hackers use "file-less" malware that operates in your computer's memory, completely bypassing traditional antivirus scans.

How to Protect Your NJ Business

Here is the minimum security stack we recommend for all our clients in Ramsey, Paramus, and Mahwah:

  • Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR): Unlike antivirus, EDR uses Artificial Intelligence to look for suspicious behavior. If a program suddenly tries to encrypt 1,000 files in 5 seconds, EDR kills it instantly.
  • Immutable Backups: The first thing a hacker does is delete your backups so you have to pay. We use "immutable" cloud storage that literally cannot be deleted or altered by anyone—even us—for a set period.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): 99% of password hacks are stopped simply by requiring a code from your phone.
  • DNS Filtering: We block your employees from accidentally clicking on malicious websites.
